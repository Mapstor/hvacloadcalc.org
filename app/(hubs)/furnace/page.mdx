# Furnace Sizing, AFUE, and Selection

Furnace sizing is the part of HVAC where most contractor quotes drift highest. A common pattern is sizing for the worst single hour of the worst winter day, then adding a 25% safety margin — producing a furnace 50 to 80 percent larger than the home actually needs. The consequences: short-cycling, hot-then-cold comfort swings, premature heat exchanger failures, and noticeably higher fuel bills than a correctly-sized system would produce.

This section covers correct furnace sizing per ACCA Manual J, what AFUE actually measures, the practical differences between 80 and 95 AFUE units, and the diagnostic signs that an existing furnace is oversized.
