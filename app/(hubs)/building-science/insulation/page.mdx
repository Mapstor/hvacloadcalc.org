# Insulation Reference

Insulation slows conductive heat flow through the building envelope. R-value rates that slowing on a steady-state basis: higher R = more resistance, less heat flow. The envelope's overall thermal performance is the weighted sum of every surface's R-value, plus the air leakage that bypasses insulation entirely.

This section covers insulation R-values by climate zone, the major insulation types and their R-per-inch, and the air-sealing work that must accompany any insulation upgrade.
