# Heat Pump Sizing, Performance, and Reference

Heat pumps now match or beat gas furnaces in most US climates, but sizing them correctly is harder than sizing a single-stage air conditioner. Compressor capacity falls as outdoor temperature falls, the system has a balance point below which auxiliary heat carries part of the load, and the wrong size produces either short-cycling in mild weather or aux-heat-dominated bills in cold weather.

This section covers the methodology for sizing heat pumps, the performance metrics that matter (HSPF2, COP, balance point, NEEP cold-climate data), and the troubleshooting patterns for the most common failure modes.

## What this section covers

- **Sizing methodology** — compute heating and cooling loads, then translate to capacity at the local 99% design temperature
- **Auxiliary heat** — when it should run, when it shouldn't, and what staging really controls
- **Cold-climate operation** — balance point, defrost cycles, NEEP cold-climate database
- **Performance metrics** — HSPF2, SEER2, COP, EER and which applies when
- **Troubleshooting** — short cycling, runs constantly, won't reach setpoint, icing
