# Manual D Residential Duct Design

ACCA Manual D is the methodology for designing residential duct systems. The goal is delivering the design airflow to each room within the static pressure budget the equipment can sustain, while keeping noise, leakage, and balance within reasonable limits. Done correctly, the result is even temperatures across the home, low noise, and equipment that can actually hit its rated performance.

This section covers the friction-rate worksheet at the core of Manual D, equivalent length calculations for fittings, static pressure budgets, the practical differences between flex and metal duct, and what to look for in a contractor's duct design.
